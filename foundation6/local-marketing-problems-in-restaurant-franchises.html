Local Marketing Problems for Restaurant Franchises

Restaurant franchises still struggle with corporate in their local marketing spend.

Topics: Restaurant Branding, Local Marketing Spend, Restaurant Franchises

The biggest marketing problem facing restaurant franchises is striking an appropriate balance between national brand advertising and local marketing for individual stores and franchisees.

While large-scale campaigns create general brand consciousness, individual franchisees need effective tools to drive results in their local markets. For most franchises, marketing efforts typically flow from the top down to ensure individual stores maintain brand standards. But, this arrangement often leaves franchisees with limited options that do not directly target and motivate their core customers.

Missing Tools

Because of this ongoing disconnect, It is not uncommon for franchisees to feel dissatisfied with their local marketing options and franchisors to feel frustrated by their franchisees’ lack of local marketing efforts. The truth is, without well designed tools that can satisfy the needs of both franchisors and franchisees, each will likely continue to feel dissatisfied and frustrated.

To solve this problem, one must identify the needs of both stakeholders. Franchisors are invested in growing brand awareness and recognition at a macro level. The key to achieving this objective is maintaining consistency to create brand familiarity, which is why ensuring brand standards are met is important. On the other hand, franchisees are competing in individual local markets, each with its own nuances and idiosyncrasies. The key to achieving their objective is connecting directly with the customers that they serve on a daily basis.

Striking a Balance

The objectives of both franchisors and franchisees can be achieved, but franchisors must feel confident that local marketing is consistent with larger brand strategies and franchisees must feel confident that their money and efforts are driving results at their individual stores. Further, any effective marketing tool that intends to work for both franchisors and franchisees must be able to accomplish the goals of each with minimal effort and, preferably, at minimal cost.
