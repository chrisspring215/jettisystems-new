What is Customer Loyalty?

The real meaning of customer loyalty and what it means for your restaurant.

Topics: Restaurant Upselling, Full Service Restaurants, Quick Service Restaurants, Loyalty Programs

Customer loyalty has evolved from a simple catchphrase to that of an industry buzzword. It's tossed around in marketing departments as if its meaning were as clear as day. “We need to improve customer loyalty” is an all too common directive from restaurant executives, but what does “customer loyalty” actually mean?

The answer, of course, is not entirely well understood. Even many “rewards” companies think they know, but it’s obvious that they have no idea. In our own view, the concept of customer loyalty can be summarized as follows:

A customer's deeply felt affinity or preference for one company or product over all the others.

Sounds simple, right? The complexity lies in how and why customers develop an affinity or preference and what it means in a practical terms for your restaurant.

To illustrate, allow me to tell you a quick personal story of how I developed loyalty to a particular business. By my house, there are about 4 convenience stores- the kind of brand-less, neighborhood places that sell chips, candy, beer, wine and pizza by the slice. One Friday after work in April, I decided to stop in to one of the stores to grab some supplies. This particular store was no different or more convenient than any of the others, it just happened to be in my line of sight.

After making a purchase and getting my change back from the clerk, I left. It was a routine and unmemorable experience. Several months passed and I did not return to this particular store, though, I'm sure I stopped by other convenience stores in the neighborhood...

It's now mid-July and I needed to get some beer for a party. For whatever reason, I decided to go back to that original store. Upon checking out, the guy behind the counter asked me an odd question. He said, “You don't happen to drive a black Jeep, do you?” At first, I was puzzled, but told him something like “Well, it's actually forest green, but why do you ask?” He said “Oh, I remember you from the last time you came in- I gave you the wrong change!” Before I knew it, he opened up the drawer and pulled out the change he was supposed to have given me 3 months prior and stapled to it was my original receipt. I was stunned- that someone who I had only met once (a) remembered me, and (b) was honest enough to give me back my lost change blew me away.

Today, I no longer view this particular store as a nameless, brand-less purveyor of party supplies, but as a place that I will now elevate above all the others. You may be asking what this story has to do with customer loyalty. What I will tell you is that it can take as little as one moment to develop a customer's loyalty for life. As restauranteurs, you often instruct your staff that it only takes one bad experience to lose a customer for life, well, the opposite is just as true. That's what our company is all about. We can help you create that one moment- or series of moments- that delight your guests and earn you their loyalty for life.
